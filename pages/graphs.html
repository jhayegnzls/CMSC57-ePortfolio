Graphs

What have I learned?

As someone who generally enjoys topics with strong visual components, I initially found the study of graphs appealing and engaging. The topic began with promise and seemed well-suited to my learning preferences. However, as we delved into more complex subtopics, I found myself increasingly confused and began questioning the purpose and utility of some of the concepts.

Despite the challenges and moments of confusion, I experienced a great deal of satisfaction when the ideas finally started to make sense. The process of working through difficult graph problems became rewarding, and I developed a genuine appreciation for the structure and logic involved in this area of mathematics. Ultimately, I realized that Graph Theory, though initially overwhelming, is both intellectually stimulating and highly applicable.


Real Life applications

What made this topic particularly meaningful for me was discovering its relevance in everyday tasks. For instance, I recently applied concepts from graph theory when planning out my day. By treating each location I needed to visit as a vertex and the paths between them as edges, I was able to determine the most efficient route. This helped minimize travel time and ensured I could move from one place to another with optimal efficiency.

This simple yet practical example demonstrated how graph theory can be used to solve real-world problems involving routing, scheduling, and optimization. It gave me a new perspective on how seemingly abstract mathematical concepts can have tangible, day-to-day applications.
