Algebraic Structure and Groups

What have I learned?

When we began diving into Algebraic Sets and Groups, to be honest, I was lost from the start. However, when we illustrated concepts in class, like rotating and flipping a square, it actually began to make sense.

I learned that an algebraic structure is basically a set with an operation that follows certain rules. One key type we focused on was a group, which has to follow four main properties: closure, associativity, identity, and inverses. We saw how these apply through examples like square symmetries, integers under addition, and matrices.

Additionally, we also practiced solving for the GCD and modulo inverses, which helped me understand how these ideas work with actual numbers. At first, it felt abstract and a bit confusing, but once the pieces clicked, I started to appreciate how everything fits together and why it matters.
